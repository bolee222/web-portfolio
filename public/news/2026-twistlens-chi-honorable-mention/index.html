TwistLens Project Receives Honorable Mention Award at ACM CHI 2026

TwistLens was recognized with an Honorable Mention Award (Top 5%) at ACM CHI 2026.

TwistLens: A Docent-Informed Image Transformation to Create Previews That Prompt Anticipation and Interpretive Experiences Before Museum Visits received an Honorable Mention Award (Top 5%) at the ACM CHI Conference on Human Factors in Computing Systems 2026.

The project, by Thao Phuong Vu and Bokyung Lee, explores how docent-informed AI image transformations can create museum previews that encourage anticipation and interpretation without revealing too much of the original experience. Its EchoLens and DecoyLens strategies turn pre-visit media into cues for curiosity, surprise, and active meaning-making.

This recognition highlights the project’s contribution to museum experience design, generative AI, and human-computer interaction.
